	Osaka Castle, or "Osaka-jo" in Japanese, is unquestionably one of Japan's most well-known landmarks, and with good reason. 
	       With a nearly 450-year history, this five-story castle is one of the most visually spectacular creations in the country.

WHERE TO FIND

Osaka Castle is located in Kyobashi, a business district to the east of Osaka that is also one of the city's most modern districts. The surrounding area appears to be a city planner's model landscape of lawns, forest, great bowls, domes, and gleaming, mirror-clad buildings when viewed from the castle. The castle appears an age away from the highrises, an age when the city was born. Five different train stations surround Osaka Castle, but Morinomiya or Osakajokoen Station provide the best access. Visitors can access the castle through the gardens from either station, and it's definitely worth it for curious visitors to arrive by one and depart by the other. It is highly recommended that you spend at least two to three hours at Osaka Castle, as this will give you enough time to take in the scenery, both man-made and natural.

WHY VISIT

Osaka Castle (Osaka-jo in Japanese) is the city's most popular tourist attraction, and for good reason: it's a stunning sight, rising high above the city's east side and commanding the skyline. The original castle was built in 1583 and was repeatedly attacked and destroyed, most recently during WWII air raids. The current castle was built from concrete and completed in 1995. Even if you don't intend to enter the castle, the expansive park surrounding Osaka Castle (known in Japanese as Osakajo-koen) is well worth a visit. On the park's grounds, there are frequently street performers and free concerts. The park is packed with people having hanami (cherry blossom viewing) parties when the cherries bloom in late March and early April. It's a lot of fun, and we encourage you to join in. The Naniwa-no-Miya Palace Ruins, arguably Osaka's best secret park, are only a short walk from Osaka Castle. It's a fantastic spot for unwinding.
