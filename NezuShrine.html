If you want to relax your mind and connect with nature while getting away from the toxicity of the city, Nezu Shrine in Tokyo's Bunkyo ward near Ueno Park is the place to go. Nezu is more secluded than the capital's larger Shinto shrines and Buddhist temples, making it a great off-the-beaten-path destination for travelers seeking peace and quiet.

WHERE TO FIND

The Nezu Shrine is located in Nezu, Bunkyo City, in Tokyo, Japan's capital. It is one of Japan's oldest shrines surrounded by lush greenery, with carp ponds, tunnels of small shrine arches, and elegant, brightly colored wooden structures that reflect Japanese culture in all its age and beauty.Given that, The Nezu-jinja Shrine is certainly one of the most attractive and unquestionably one of Tokyo's most photogenic shrines. The culturally significant site is only a few minutes' walk from the Chiyoda Line's Nezu Station and the Namboku Line's Todaimae Station.

WHY VISIT

Visitors to Nezu Shrine have little reason to be sad now that the cherry blossoms have faded. Thousands of azalea bushes bloom in late April and early May on the grounds of the shrine. The long procession of torii gates that wind over the low hills on the shrine precincts stand out beautifully in contrast to the various shades of purples and pinks. Nezu Shrine has earned the title of "Tokyo's Most Beautiful Shrine" thanks to this colorful festival. A trip to Japan, which begins in Tokyo at the beginning of May, is recommended for nature lovers. You'll avoid the crush of cherry blossom crowds while still making it to Nezu in time to see the azaleas.
